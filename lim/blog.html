WALLS HAVE EARS:

The great mighty strong Big Ben (Magana himuselefu) shares his valuable wisdom and experience with the national youth chess team, a week before they traveled to Eindhoven, Netherlands, to represent Kenya in the World Youth Chess Olympiad (12th-19th August 2023) featuring more than 65 youth teams from around the world. The Eight Attentive Ears belong to Bernice Wambui, Kyle Kuka, Paul Olando and Hawi Kaloki, four of the fastest rising youths in Kenya. At this point in Kenya's chess history, our seasoned, experienced senior players carry the noble responsibility of mentoring the upcoming youths to walk in the right paths, to avoid the same mistakes, errors, trappings, and pitfalls that were experienced by their Great Predecessors. To this end, I can also confirm and rubber stamp the dedication and commitment of our senior players who have now appreciated the enormously convincing efforts by the young players themuselefs, strongly supported by their wonderful awesome parents and guardians. A truly unprecedented phenomenon! Finally, I must note that the Transformative Philosophy currently driving and shaping our local Kenyan chess fraternity is now an Open Secret: Kindness, Respect, Humility, Discipline, and Responsibility. Gens Una Sumus...
